How are the scores calculated?

Synergy score teal: how well a suggested card fits your deck’s themes and roles.

{% if section == "new" %}

score = 2 × matched tags + 1 ÷ log10(EDHREC rank + 10)
More theme/role matches and a lower EDHREC rank both raise this number. Normalized to a 1–10 scale across all suggestions for this deck.

{% else %}

score = 2 × theme matches + 1.5 × role-gap fills + 1 ÷ log10(EDHREC rank + 10)
Cards that cover gaps in your deck’s role spread and rank well on EDHREC score highest. Normalized to a 1–10 scale across all suggestions for this deck (the relative ranking between cards matters more than the absolute value).

{% endif %}

Replaceability score amber: how swappable a current deck card is for this specific suggestion (1–10).

score = role overlap weight + CMC saved bonus + quality adjustment
Higher means the card shares more roles with the suggestion and/or has a high CMC relative to what would replace it, making it a stronger swap-out candidate. Double-faced cards are penalized to reflect the utility both sides provide.

Note: the same deck card may show a different replaceability score in New Cards vs General Upgrades; this is expected. The score is not a fixed property of the card; it measures how well that card fits as a cut for the specific incoming suggestion. A card with high CMC and strong role overlap will score higher as a swap target for some suggestions than others.

Price ratio filter: swap targets are chosen based on the suggestion’s price range.

Under $2: the swap target must also be under $2 (cheap suggestions stay in the cheap range).
$2 and up: 0.5 ≤ suggestion / cut ≤ 2.0 — e.g. a $5 suggestion pairs with targets priced $2.50–$10.
Suggestions above the deck’s per-card budget ceiling lose swap targets but still appear for reference.

Cards that pass the synergy check but fail the price ratio (or have too few role-matching cut candidates) appear in the Possible Upgrades tab without swap targets.

Heads up: These are algorithmic suggestions, not prescriptions. The tool weighs role overlap, theme tag coverage, CMC efficiency, and EDHREC popularity, but it cannot account for everything: specific combo lines, political utility, pet cards, budget constraints, regional card availability, power-level preferences, or subtle synergies that aren’t captured in role tags. A high replaceability score on a target card means the algorithm thinks it’s a reasonable swap candidate; it doesn’t mean you must cut it. Always apply your own judgment before making changes.
